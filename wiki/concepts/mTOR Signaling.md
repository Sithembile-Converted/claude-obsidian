# mTOR Signaling

Navigation: [[learning/_index]] | [[Hypertrophy]] | [[Anabolic State]]

---

## Definition

mTOR (mammalian target of rapamycin) is a protein kinase that acts as the **master regulator of muscle protein synthesis and cell growth**. When activated, it tells the cell to build. When suppressed, the cell conserves or breaks down.

Understanding mTOR is understanding the molecular switch for hypertrophy.

---

## Two Complexes

| Complex | Role | Activation |
|---|---|---|
| **mTORC1** | Primary driver of protein synthesis and hypertrophy | Amino acids (leucine), insulin, mechanical load, IGF-1 |
| **mTORC2** | Supports hypertrophy and cell survival; less studied | Growth factors, mechanical load |

Both complexes are required for full hypertrophic response. Blocking mTORC1 alone reduces hypertrophy; blocking both reduces it further.

---

## How mTORC1 Triggers Protein Synthesis

1. mTORC1 phosphorylates **4E-BP1** (a translation inhibitor)
2. Phosphorylated 4E-BP1 releases **eIF4E** (initiation factor)
3. eIF4E binds the 5' cap of mRNA → cap-dependent translation begins
4. Ribosomes synthesize contractile proteins (actin, myosin, etc.)

mTORC1 also activates **S6K1**, which promotes ribosome biogenesis — meaning more ribosomes to run future protein synthesis.

---

## What Activates mTOR

- **Resistance training** — mechanical tension directly activates mTOR independent of hormones
- **Leucine** — the amino acid in protein that most potently activates mTOR; found highest in whey, eggs, meat
- **Insulin** — released in response to carbohydrates; activates PI3K/Akt → mTOR
- **IGF-1** — growth factor elevated post-exercise
- **Protein + carbs together** — the combination produces greater mTORC1 signaling than exercise alone

---

## What Opposes mTOR

**AMPK** (AMP-activated protein kinase) is the energy sensor. When cellular energy is low (fasted state, high-intensity cardio, severe calorie restriction), AMPK activates and suppresses mTOR.

This is why:
- Fasted training blunts hypertrophy vs fed training
- Extreme calorie deficits prevent muscle growth
- Excessive cardio interferes with hypertrophy

> [!key-insight] The mTOR/AMPK balance
> Optimal muscle growth requires mTOR dominance during training and recovery windows. This is achieved through adequate calories, protein around training, and avoiding extended fasted periods near lifting sessions.

---

## Practical Implications

- **Eat protein before and after training** to maximize leucine-driven mTOR activation
- **Include carbs post-workout** to drive insulin-mediated mTOR signaling
- **Don't train fasted** for hypertrophy goals — feed the mTOR pathway
- **8 weeks of consistent training** further upregulates Akt/mTORC1 phosphorylation — the pathway becomes more efficient over time
