# Anabolic State

Navigation: [[learning/_index]] | [[Hypertrophy]] | [[mTOR Signaling]] | [[Muscle Building Nutrition]]

---

## Definition

An anabolic state is a physiological condition in which the body is in **net tissue building mode** — protein synthesis exceeds protein breakdown, hormones favor growth, and resources are directed toward muscle repair and growth.

Its opposite is a **catabolic state**: tissue breakdown dominates, driven by cortisol, energy deficit, sleep deprivation, or overtraining.

---

## How to Enter the Anabolic State

The anabolic state is triggered by the combination of:

1. **Resistance training stimulus** — activates [[mTOR Signaling]], triggers satellite cell proliferation
2. **Protein availability** — leucine-rich protein activates mTORC1 directly
3. **Calorie surplus** — even small surplus (200-300 cal) signals abundance and supports MPS
4. **Post-workout insulin spike** — carbs + protein post-training amplifies mTOR signaling

> [!key-insight] Training creates the signal; nutrition provides the materials
> You cannot build without both. Training without sufficient protein/calories yields adaptation without growth. Eating without training yields fat storage, not muscle.

---

## How to Stay in the Anabolic State

### 1. Sleep (Highest Leverage)

Sleep is the most anabolic period of the day.

- **70%** of daily growth hormone (GH) pulses happen during non-REM deep sleep
- One night of sleep deprivation drops testosterone by **24%**
- Restricting sleep to 5 hrs/night for 1 week lowers testosterone by 15% — equivalent to 10-15 years of natural aging
- 7-9 hours/night is the minimum for optimized anabolic hormone environment
- The real muscle-building happens in the 22-23 hours outside the gym

**If sleep is poor, training hard is counterproductive.** You create the stimulus but deny the recovery.

### 2. Progressive Overload

Without new challenge, the muscle sees no reason to grow. Plateau = exiting the adaptive state. See [[Progressive Overload]].

### 3. Consistent Protein Distribution

- 20-40g protein every 3-4 hours throughout the day
- Keeps amino acids available for MPS continuously
- Pre-sleep casein protein (cottage cheese, Greek yogurt) extends overnight MPS

### 4. Manage Cortisol

Cortisol is the primary catabolic hormone. Chronically elevated cortisol breaks down muscle:

- **Sleep deprivation** elevates cortisol
- **Under-eating** (aggressive deficit) elevates cortisol
- **Psychological stress** elevates cortisol
- **Overtraining** elevates cortisol

Managing life stress is a legitimate part of muscle building.

### 5. Avoid Extended Fasted States Near Training

High AMPK activity (fasted state) suppresses mTOR. Train fed, or at minimum consume protein beforehand.

### 6. Deload Regularly

Every 4-8 weeks, reduce volume by 40-50% for one week. Prevents accumulated fatigue from suppressing anabolic hormones and masking progress.

## Anabolic Hormones

| Hormone | Role | How to Optimize |
|---|---|---|
| **Testosterone** | Drives muscle protein synthesis, satellite cell activation | Sleep 7-9 hrs, lift heavy, adequate dietary fat, manage stress |
| **Growth Hormone (GH)** | Stimulates protein synthesis, fat mobilization | Deep sleep (non-REM), high-intensity training, low blood sugar before bed |
| **IGF-1** | Mediates GH effects in muscle, activates mTOR | GH secretion, adequate protein and calories |
| **Insulin** | Anabolic at physiological levels, drives amino acid uptake | Post-workout carbs + protein, avoid chronically high levels |

## The Daily Anabolic Cycle

```
Morning:       Feed protein early — end overnight fast
Pre-workout:   Carbs + protein 1-4 hrs before training
Training:      Mechanical tension activates mTOR
Post-workout:  20-30g protein + carbs within 1 hour
Evening meals: Protein every 3-4 hrs throughout day
Pre-sleep:     Casein protein — feeds MPS overnight
Sleep:         7-9 hrs — GH pulses, testosterone maintained, repair happens
```

---

## What Breaks the Anabolic State

| Disruptor | Why It's Catabolic |
|---|---|
| Sleep deprivation | Cuts GH and testosterone, raises cortisol |
| Aggressive calorie deficit | AMPK suppresses mTOR; insufficient building materials |
| Skipping protein meals | MPS falls without consistent amino acid availability |
| Overtraining | Elevated cortisol, suppressed testosterone |
| Chronic stress | Cortisol is chronically catabolic |
| Excessive alcohol | Suppresses testosterone, disrupts sleep architecture |
