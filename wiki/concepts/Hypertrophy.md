# Hypertrophy

Navigation: [[learning/_index]] | [[Hypertrophy Training]] | [[Anabolic State]] | [[Muscle Building Nutrition]]

---

## Definition

Muscle hypertrophy is the increase in the size and cross-sectional area of individual muscle fibers, resulting in visibly larger muscles. It occurs when **muscle protein synthesis (MPS) consistently exceeds muscle protein breakdown (MPB)** over time.

It is not the same as strength (neural adaptations drive early strength gains). True hypertrophy requires weeks-to-months of consistent stimulus.

---

## The Three Stimuli

All three can trigger hypertrophy independently; combining them is optimal.

| Stimulus | Mechanism | Training Style |
|---|---|---|
| **Mechanical tension** | Load stretches and contracts fibers under stress | Heavy compound lifts, progressive overload |
| **Metabolic stress** | Metabolite accumulation (lactate, H+) triggers hormonal and cellular responses | High-rep sets, pump training, short rest periods |
| **Muscle damage** | Micro-tears in fibers activate satellite cells and inflammatory repair cascade | Eccentric emphasis, novel exercises, full ROM |

> [!key-insight] The primary driver
> Mechanical tension is the most potent and most reliable stimulus. Metabolic stress and muscle damage amplify it but cannot replace it.

---

## The Molecular Mechanism

The signal cascade from workout to new muscle:

```
Resistance exercise
    ↓
Mechanical tension detected by muscle fibers
    ↓
mTORC1 activated (via PI3K/Akt pathway + amino acids via Ragulator complex)
    ↓
4E-BP1 phosphorylated → releases eIF4E → cap-dependent mRNA translation begins
    ↓
Ribosomes synthesize new contractile proteins (actin, myosin)
    ↓
Satellite cells proliferate and fuse with damaged fibers
    ↓
Net protein synthesis > protein breakdown → fiber grows
```

See [[mTOR Signaling]] for the full molecular detail.

## Types of Hypertrophy

**Myofibrillar hypertrophy** — growth in the actual contractile proteins (actin and myosin). Increases both size and strength. Driven by heavy, lower-rep training.

**Sarcoplasmic hypertrophy** — expansion of the fluid and glycogen stores surrounding myofibrils. Increases size more than strength. Driven by higher-rep, pump-style training.

In practice, both occur simultaneously. The ratio shifts depending on training style.

---

## How Long It Takes

- First 4-8 weeks: mostly neural adaptations (strength without much size)
- Weeks 8-16: visible hypertrophy begins
- Ongoing: 0.5-1 lb of lean mass per month is realistic for natural trainees
- Requires months-to-years of compounding progressive overload

---

## What Stops Hypertrophy

- **No progressive overload** — the muscle adapts and stops growing without new stimulus
- **Insufficient protein** — no building blocks for MPS
- **Calorie deficit** — body prioritizes energy over tissue building
- **Poor sleep** — 70% of GH is secreted during deep sleep; sleep deprivation cuts testosterone 24%
- **Overtraining** — elevated cortisol suppresses anabolic hormones
- **High AMPK activity** — fasted training, excessive cardio; AMPK opposes mTOR
