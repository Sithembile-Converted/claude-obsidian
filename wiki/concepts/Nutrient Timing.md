# Nutrient Timing

Navigation: [[learning/_index]] | [[Muscle Building Nutrition]] | [[Anabolic State]]

---

## Definition

Nutrient timing is the strategic scheduling of food intake around training and throughout the day to maximize muscle protein synthesis, performance, and recovery.

> [!key-insight] Hierarchy of importance
> **Total daily intake > protein distribution > peri-workout timing.** Timing optimizes; it does not compensate for insufficient total protein or calories. Get the fundamentals right first.

---

## Pre-Workout Nutrition

**Window:** 1-4 hours before training

**Goal:** Maximize energy for training, prime amino acid availability, prevent catabolism during session

**Composition:**
- **Carbs (priority):** primary fuel source; provide performance energy
- **Protein (moderate):** 20-40g to prime MPS; protein takes longer to digest so earlier timing is better
- **Fat (low):** slows digestion; avoid large amounts close to training
- **Fiber (low):** can cause GI distress during training

**Practical meals by timing:**

| Time Before Training | Meal |
|---|---|
| 3-4 hours | Full meal: chicken breast + brown rice + vegetables |
| 2 hours | Medium meal: oats + whey protein + banana |
| 1 hour | Small meal: Greek yogurt + fruit, or banana + protein shake |
| 30 min | Protein shake only (or nothing if stomach sensitive) |

---

## Post-Workout Nutrition

**Window:** within 30-60 minutes (up to 2 hours is practically fine)

**Goal:** Spike MPS via leucine + insulin, replenish glycogen, shift from catabolic to anabolic

**Composition:**
- **Protein:** 20-30g high-quality protein (whey is ideal — fast absorbing, high leucine)
- **Carbs:** fast-digesting carbs to restore glycogen and spike insulin (anabolic signal)
- **Ratio:** ~2:1 to 3:1 carbs to protein

**The anabolic window — what the evidence says:**
- Meta-analysis of 23 RCTs: nutrient intake within 1 hour of training shows small but statistically significant benefit to hypertrophy
- The window is real, but not as narrow as once believed — within 2 hours is fine
- If you ate a pre-workout meal 1-2 hours before training, the window is less critical (amino acids still circulating)
- **Total daily protein intake matters more than exact timing**

**Practical post-workout meals:**

| Speed | Option |
|---|---|
| Fast (gym bag) | Whey protein shake + banana |
| Home within 30 min | Greek yogurt + oats + honey |
| Home meal | Rice + chicken breast + vegetables |

## Daily Protein Distribution

**The most impactful timing strategy.**

- Spread 20-40g protein every 3-4 hours throughout the day
- This distribution pattern maximizes daily MPS rates vs. front-loading or back-loading protein
- Source: ISSN guidelines — 0.25-0.40g/kg per dose every 3-4 hours

**Why:** MPS has a ceiling per meal. Beyond ~40g, excess amino acids are oxidized rather than used for building. Frequent moderate doses keep MPS elevated throughout the day.

**4-5 meals/day with 30-40g protein each beats 2 large meals.**

---

## Pre-Sleep Protein

**Window:** 30-60 minutes before bed

**Goal:** Feed overnight MPS during the 7-9 hours of sleep (the most anabolic period)

**Protein type:** Casein — slow-digesting, releases amino acids over 5-7 hours

**Sources:** Cottage cheese (best), Greek yogurt, casein powder

**Research:** 40g casein before sleep increases overnight MPS without affecting fat loss markers.

> [!key-insight] Don't fast overnight
> Your body is doing most of its repair during sleep. A casein bolus before bed is the simplest optimization most people overlook.

## Full Day Timing Framework

```
Wake up (7am)        Protein-rich breakfast — 30-40g protein
                     Break overnight fast quickly

Mid-morning (10am)   Snack if needed — Greek yogurt / eggs
                     Keep amino acids available

Pre-workout (12pm)   Full meal — carbs + protein, 2-3 hrs before
                     e.g. chicken + rice

Training (2-3pm)     Resistance training session

Post-workout (3:30)  20-30g protein + fast carbs within 1 hour
                     e.g. whey shake + banana

Dinner (6-7pm)       Protein-rich meal — 40-50g protein
                     e.g. salmon + sweet potato

Pre-sleep (9:30pm)   Casein protein — 30-40g
                     e.g. cottage cheese or casein shake

Sleep (10pm)         7-9 hours — GH secretion, testosterone, repair
```

---

## What to Avoid

- **Long fasted periods near training** — AMPK suppresses mTOR; trains the body to use muscle as fuel
- **Skipping post-workout nutrition** — misses the elevated mTOR window
- **All protein in 1-2 meals** — MPS ceiling wastes amino acids; spread it out
- **High fat/high fiber pre-workout** — slows digestion, reduces performance
