# Muscle Building Nutrition

Navigation: [[learning/_index]] | [[Hypertrophy]] | [[Anabolic State]] | [[Nutrient Timing]]

---

## The Three Requirements

To build muscle, your diet must provide:

1. **Sufficient protein** — amino acids as building blocks for MPS
2. **Calorie surplus** — energy to fund the anabolic process
3. **Adequate carbohydrates** — fuel for training and glycogen replenishment

Miss any one of these consistently and hypertrophy stalls.

---

## Macros

### Protein
- **Target:** 1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight per day (0.7-1g per lb)
- 2g/kg is the practical standard for most natural lifters
- Going above 2.2g/kg produces no additional benefit and just adds calories
- Distribute across 4-5 meals at 20-40g per meal for maximum daily MPS

### Calories
- **Surplus:** 200-300 calories above maintenance (lean bulk)
- Aggressive surpluses (500+ cal over) add proportionally more fat without significantly more muscle
- Slow bulk = more lean mass ratio over time
- Track using TDEE calculator as a starting point, then adjust based on weekly weight change

### Carbohydrates
- **Target:** 45-50% of total calories
- Carbs drive training performance, pump, and recovery more than any other macro
- Glycogen replenishment post-workout is critical for next session quality
- Don't go low-carb for hypertrophy goals — it compromises training intensity

### Fats
- **Target:** 20-25% of total calories
- Essential for testosterone production — don't cut below 20%
- Source from unsaturated fats: olive oil, nuts, avocado, fatty fish
- Omega-3s (salmon, mackerel) reduce muscle inflammation and may enhance MPS

### Example Macro Split (for 80kg person)
- Calories: ~2800-3000/day (maintenance ~2500-2700 + 200-300 surplus)
- Protein: 160g (2g/kg)
- Carbs: 340-380g (45-50%)
- Fat: 70-80g (20-25%)

## Best Protein Sources

### Animal (Complete Proteins — All Essential Amino Acids)

| Food | Protein per 100g | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Chicken breast | 32g | Lean, versatile, high leucine |
| Turkey | 30g | Lean, similar to chicken |
| Tuna | 25g | Convenient, low fat |
| Lean beef | 26g | Also provides creatine, zinc, iron |
| Salmon | 25g | Protein + omega-3s for recovery |
| Eggs | 13g | Complete profile, high bioavailability, cheap |
| Greek yogurt | 10g/100g (~20g/cup) | Casein + whey, great snack |
| Cottage cheese | 11g | Casein-dominant, ideal pre-sleep |
| Whey protein | 20-25g/scoop | Fast-absorbing, high leucine, ideal post-workout |

> [!key-insight] Leucine is the trigger
> Leucine is the amino acid that most directly activates mTOR. Highest leucine content: whey protein, chicken, beef, eggs. Aim for 2-3g leucine per meal to maximally trigger MPS.

### Plant (Incomplete — except soy)

| Food | Protein per cup (cooked) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Soy / Tofu / Edamame | 20g | Only complete plant protein; research shows comparable MPS to whey |
| Lentils | 18g | Also high iron |
| Chickpeas | 15g + 45g carbs | High fiber, good for body composition |
| Black beans | 15g | |
| Quinoa | 8g | Complete protein grain |

> [!note] Plant proteins
> Most plant proteins are incomplete (missing one or more essential amino acids) and have lower leucine content. Combine sources (e.g. rice + beans) or use soy/quinoa. May need 20-30% more total protein to hit same MPS as animal protein.

## Best Carbohydrate Sources

**Pre-workout (slow-digesting):**
- Oats, brown rice, sweet potato, whole grain bread, whole grain pasta
- Provide sustained energy without blood sugar crash

**Post-workout (fast-digesting):**
- White rice, banana, fruit, white potato
- Rapid glycogen replenishment, spike insulin (anabolic signal)

---

## Best Fat Sources

- Olive oil, avocado (monounsaturated)
- Nuts and seeds — almonds, walnuts, chia (polyunsaturated)
- Fatty fish — salmon, mackerel, sardines (omega-3)
- Eggs (yolks)

---

## Foods to Limit

- **Ultra-processed foods** — low nutrient density, poor satiety per calorie
- **Excessive alcohol** — suppresses testosterone, disrupts sleep, interferes with MPS
- **High-fat meals around training** — fat slows digestion; bad pre/post-workout
- **High-fiber foods pre-workout** — can cause GI distress during training
