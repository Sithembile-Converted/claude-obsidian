## Summary

Comprehensive web research sweep (2026-04-22) across 7 topics covering the full science of muscle hypertrophy: molecular mechanisms, training protocols, nutrition, and nutrient timing. Synthesized from Frontiers in Medicine, PMC, PubMed, NASM, Harvard Health, and ISSN guidelines.

---

## Key Insights

1. **mTOR is the master switch.** Resistance training + leucine-rich protein activates mTORC1, which triggers protein synthesis. The combination produces greater hypertrophic signaling than either alone.

2. **Volume is the primary training variable.** 10-20 sets per muscle group per week is the evidence-based range. Diminishing returns above 20 sets; counterproductive above 16 sets per session.

3. **Sleep is the most anabolic variable.** 70% of daily GH pulses happen during non-REM deep sleep. One night of deprivation cuts testosterone 24%. 7-9 hours is the minimum.

4. **Any rep range works near failure.** 6-30 reps all produce comparable hypertrophy when trained to 0-3 RIR. The 8-12 range is efficient because it balances tension and metabolic stress.

5. **Protein distribution beats timing.** 20-40g protein every 3-4 hours all day maximizes daily MPS rates more than meal timing specifics.

6. **The anabolic window is real but forgiving.** Within 2 hours post-training is practically sufficient. Pre-workout protein reduces the urgency of immediate post-workout feeding.

7. **200-300 calorie surplus beats aggressive bulking.** Lean bulk = more muscle-to-fat gain ratio over time. Aggressive surpluses add disproportionate fat.
