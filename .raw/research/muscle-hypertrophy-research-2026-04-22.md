# Muscle Hypertrophy Research — Compiled 2026-04-22

## 1. What Is Hypertrophy

Muscle hypertrophy is the increase in muscle fiber size and cross-sectional area. It occurs when muscle protein synthesis (MPS) exceeds muscle protein breakdown (MPB) over time.

Three primary stimuli:
- **Mechanical tension** — load placed on muscle fibers during resistance training
- **Metabolic stress** — accumulation of metabolites (lactate, H+, Pi) during high-rep training
- **Muscle damage** — micro-tears in fibers that trigger repair and growth response

## 2. The mTOR Pathway (Molecular Mechanism)

mTOR (mammalian target of rapamycin) is the master regulator:
- Resistance training activates mTORC1 (complex 1) → triggers protein synthesis
- mTORC2 also contributes; combined inhibition reduces hypertrophy more than mTORC1 alone
- mTORC1 phosphorylates 4E-BP1 → releases eIF4E → initiates cap-dependent mRNA translation
- Protein + carb feeding post-exercise enhances mTORC1 signaling beyond exercise alone
- 8 weeks of resistance training further upregulates Akt/mTORC1 phosphorylation

AMPK (energy sensor) opposes mTOR — high AMPK activity (fasted state, excessive cardio) blunts hypertrophy. Balance between mTOR and AMPK is key.

## 3. Training: How to Trigger Hypertrophy

### Volume
- 10-20 sets per muscle group per week is the evidence-based sweet spot
- Competitive physique athletes: ~40-70 reps per session, minimum 2x/week per muscle
- Diminishing returns above ~20 sets/week; >16 sets/session may inversely affect gains
- Progressive overload: increases should stay within 10% per week

### Intensity & Rep Range
- 8-12 reps at 60-80% 1RM is the classic hypertrophy range
- Research shows 6-30 reps all produce hypertrophy when trained near failure (1-3 RIR)
- Training to failure or near-failure is important — leaving many reps in reserve reduces stimulus

### Frequency
- 2+ days per week per muscle group clearly beats 1 day
- When per-session volume exceeds 15 sets, split into more sessions
- 3-5 training days per week total is optimal for most

### Progressive Overload (Key Principle)
- Without progression, growth plateaus; prolonged decrease in load causes atrophy
- Add weight, reps, or sets over time
- Max 10% increase per week to minimize injury risk
- Track lifts to ensure progression is happening

## 4. Staying in the Hypertrophic State

### Sleep (Most Underrated Variable)
- 70% of daily GH (growth hormone) pulses occur during non-REM deep sleep
- One night of sleep deprivation reduces testosterone by 24%
- Restricting to 5 hrs/night for 1 week decreases daytime testosterone 15% — equivalent to 10-15 years of aging
- 7-9 hours/night is the evidence-based minimum
- Real muscle-building happens in the 22-23 hrs outside the gym

### Recovery
- Muscles require 48-72 hrs recovery per muscle group
- Overtraining suppresses testosterone and elevates cortisol (catabolic)
- Deload every 4-8 weeks: reduce volume by 40-50%
- High cortisol (stress, under-eating, under-sleeping) drives catabolism

### Consistency
- Hypertrophy is a slow process: 0.5-1 lb lean mass/month is realistic for naturals
- Compounding gains require months-to-years of consistent progressive overload

## 5. Foods That Build Muscle

### Best Protein Sources
- Chicken breast: 32g protein per 100g — lean, complete amino acid profile
- Eggs: complete protein + healthy fats, high leucine content
- Greek yogurt: ~20g protein per cup, contains casein (slow-release) + whey
- Cottage cheese: casein-dominant, great pre-sleep protein
- Lean beef: high protein + creatine + zinc + iron
- Turkey: lean, ~30g protein per 100g
- Salmon/fatty fish: protein + omega-3s (reduce muscle inflammation)
- Tuna: 25g protein per 100g, low fat
- Soy / tofu: complete plant protein, research shows comparable to whey for MPS
- Chickpeas: 15g protein + 45g carbs per cup, high fiber

### Carbohydrate Sources (Fuel Training and Recovery)
- Oats, brown rice, sweet potato, whole grains — slow-digesting, steady energy
- White rice, bananas, fruit — fast-digesting, ideal around workouts
- Carbs drive training performance, pumps, and glycogen replenishment

### Healthy Fats (Hormonal Support)
- Nuts, avocado, olive oil, fatty fish
- Fat is essential for testosterone production — don't go below 20% of calories

## 6. Macros for Muscle Building

- **Protein:** 1.6-2.2g per kg body weight per day (0.7-1g per lb)
- **Calorie surplus:** 200-300 cal above maintenance (aggressive surpluses add fat without proportionally more muscle)
- **Macro split (typical):** 45-50% carbs | 30-35% protein | 20-25% fat
- **Protein dose per meal:** 20-40g (0.25-0.40g/kg) every 3-4 hours to maximize daily MPS

## 7. Nutrient Timing

### Pre-Workout (1-4 hours before)
- Majority carbs + moderate protein
- Carbs are primary fuel; protein primes amino acid availability
- Example: chicken + rice 2-3 hrs before, or oats + protein shake 1 hr before
- Avoid high fat/fiber immediately pre-workout (slows digestion, causes discomfort)

### Post-Workout (within 30-60 minutes)
- 20-30g high-quality protein to kickstart MPS
- Carbs to replenish glycogen and enhance insulin (anabolic signal)
- The "anabolic window" is real — meta-analysis of 23 RCTs shows small but significant benefit within 1 hour
- Within 2 hours is practically fine — total daily intake matters more than exact timing

### Daily Distribution
- 20-40g protein every 3-4 hours throughout the day
- This distribution outperforms large single boluses for maximizing daily MPS
- Example: 4-5 meals/day, each with 30-40g protein

### Pre-Sleep Protein
- Casein protein (cottage cheese, Greek yogurt) before bed feeds MPS overnight
- 40g casein pre-sleep has been shown to increase overnight MPS without affecting fat loss
